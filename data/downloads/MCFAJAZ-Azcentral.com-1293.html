Quicksand guitarist accused of shoplifting, resisting arrest at Phoenix CVS

Robert Gundran, The Republic | azcentral.com Published 11:10 a.m. MT Sept. 14, 2017 | Updated 11:18 a.m. MT Sept. 14, 2017
CLOSE

When New York City-based post-hardcore band Quicksandtook to the stage of the Crescent Ballroom on Tuesday night, there was one glaring omission from the four-piece band.

Guitarist Tom Caponewas nowhere to be found.

That's because Capone, 47,was in jail on suspicion of shoplifting and resisting arrest from an incident that afternoon, according to Phoenix police.

According to a police report, Capone was observed on closed-circuit television shoplifting from the CVS pharmacy near Jefferson Street and FirstAvenue.

CVS employees said they watched Capone put multiple items 43 in all into his backpack and pockets, police said. Once he left the store, CVS employees reported the theft to a uniformedoff-duty police officer working in the plaza.

The police records did not detail what kind of items Capone is accused of stealing.

Police said in court records that the officer approached Capone and told him to stop, butCapone kept walking away.

When the officer grabbed Capone, he tried to break away from the officer and continued to resist as the officer took him to the ground and put him in handcuffs, records show.Capone was booked into Fourth AvenueJail.

A Maricopa County Superior Court commissioner released Capone on his own recognizance Wednesday morning. His next court date is scheduled for Sept. 27.

Quicksand will perform in Dallas Thursday night.
