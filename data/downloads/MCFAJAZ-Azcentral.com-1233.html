Suspect in nearly 15-hour standoff at Phoenix hotel is identified

Stephanie Morse, The Republic | azcentral.com Published 10:51 a.m. MT Nov. 1, 2017 | Updated 4:21 p.m. MT Nov. 1, 2017

Gary Minor(Photo: Maricopa County Sheriff's Office)

CONNECTTWEETLINKEDINCOMMENTEMAILMORE

The suspect in a nearly 15-hour standoff with police at a Comfort Suites hotel in Phoenix has been identified as Gary Laverne Minor, authorities said Wednesday.

The 45-year-old Surprise resident was booked into jail Tuesday evening on suspicion of crimes that include aggravated assault, endangerment, unlawful imprisonment, kidnapping,discharging a firearm at a residence andunlawfulflight from law enforcement.

The standoff was preceded by an officer attemptingto stop a truck for a stolen license platenear the intersection of 27th and Dunlap avenues at about 2:30 a.m. Tuesday.The driver fled from the vehicle and rushed into the Comfort Suites near Interstate 17 and Peoria Avenue.

Police say Minor was armed with a gun and had barricaded himself in the hotel room with his wife.

Inside theroom, the pair heard police voices outside, causing Minor's wife to want to open the door for the police, according to court documents.

Minor was holding a gun and told her not to leave, but did not point the gun at her, documents say. However,Minor's wife told police she felt that he would hurt her if she tried to leave.

Court documents also say Minor was standing behind his wife for most of the incident to use her as a human shield.

Police eventually deployed a camera into the room, court documents say.Minorgrabbed his wife with one hand whileholding his gun in the other and forced her to lead him to the camera. He then asked her to throw a pillow over the camera to block it, according to the court documents.

The wife believes Minor may have pointed the gun at her back, but he never directly threatened her, according to court documents.

During the standoff, police say Minor threatened to shoot police officers.Later during negotiations, officers heard a gunshot, promptingpolice to force entry and detain Minor and his wife at about 5 p.m.

Minor told police that he heard a loud noise, causing him to accidentally fire the gun, records say.

However, records say,his wife told police she believes he fired the gun to try to get the police officers to leave. Court documents say the gunshot endangered officerswho were standing on the other side of the door; no officers were harmed.

Minor had slight injuries and his wifewas unharmed.

Court records indicate Minor has no criminal history in Arizona.
