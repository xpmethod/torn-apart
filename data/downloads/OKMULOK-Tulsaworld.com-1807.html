Man accused of making threats against state senator in Okmulgee

  • By Harrison Grimwood Tulsa World

    Staff Writer Harrison Grimwood

  • Feb 22, 2018
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Email
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Email
  • Print
  • Save

Okmulgee County sheriffs deputies say a man came to the Sheriffs Office to ask about security for a town forum where a state senator was speaking and made threats toward the legislator.

Steven Cates, 60, reportedly came to the Sheriffs Office on Feb. 15 and inquired about the forum, which featured Sen. Roger Thompson, R-Okemah.

Tulsa World journalism makes a difference. Be part of the story. Join us. Start a digital subscription for only 99.

While Cates was speaking with deputies, he then began making threats towards the senators safety and attempted to leave in order to go to where the senator was speaking, Deputy Duston Todd said in a news release.

Deputies arrested Cates as he attempted to leave. He allegedly resisted and made threats toward officers.

Cates was arrested on complaints of assault and battery on a police officer, resisting arrest and two counts of threatening to perform an act of violence.

He was booked into the Okmulgee County Jail and was being held in lieu of a $310,000 bond.

Thompson, elected in November 2014, represents Senate District 8, which includes Okmulgee and Mcintosh counties and parts of Okfuskee and Muskogee counties.
